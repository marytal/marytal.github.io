Presenting

ePortfolio Links

If I have one regret about my University degree it’s that I will not be able to fit a Public Speaking course into my schedule. While presenting is an incredibly useful skill that would benefit the trajectory of any profession, I have had little to no practice with it.

That is why I found the Ted Talk assignment both incredibly challenging and rewarding. I first wrote out my Ted Talk in the manner with which I am most familiar, essay style. An introduction, three supporting paragraphs, and a conclusion filled 3 pages. As I read my essay aloud, trying to memorize the words, I realized how uninteresting it sounded. That is when I took a look at “Structure Your Presentation Like a Story” by Nancy Duarte.

I wanted my presentation to fit “Aristotle’s three-part story structure (beginning, middle, end)” (Duarte, 2012). I started off by “crafting the beginning” which consisted of “describing life as the audience knows it” (Duarte, 2012). I relayed my experience with choosing a career path and what criteria I used to decide – typical stuff that a typical audience member (in my target audience) would relate to, opening them up to my ideas. Then, slightly deviating from Duarte’s steps, I pointed out the flaws and the negative outcomes that can arise from following the path that I had followed – and laying out the alternatives that would ameliorate them. I hope that this was enough to jar/surprise the audience (a necessary ingredient of crafting the beginning), as my “vision” is not (yet) widely accepted as the better path.

The next step, “developing the middle”, consists of “playing up the contrast between what is and what could be” (Duarte, 2012). I feel that I could have done this better had I written out my Ted Talk with this goal in mind. Unfortunately, once all the ideas are down on paper, it is more difficult for me to make connections that I otherwise could have made. That being said, I managed to make a few comparisons between the different types of goals that each criterion would help one reach. This made my conclusions stronger and more relevant.

Finally, I believe that I managed to “make the ending powerful” with a call to action and a reitteration of my vision for my listeners.

“If you take one thing away from what I’ve told you, make it this. Life doesn’t only take place from 9:00 to 5:00, nor does it only take place from 5:00 to 9:00. In order to be happy, most people need a comfortable work-life balance. Make your balance a priority and you’ll be making your happiness a priority.” ()

My Ted Talk turned out to be engaging and interesting (according to some sources, at least) and I hope to continue to grow in my story telling and presenting abilities. Watch my Ted Talk below if you’re interested.
